Popularity of Chess

Chess boasts a steadfast and global popularity that has endured for centuries. This ancient board game's appeal lies in its intellectual challenge, strategic depth, and universal accessibility. Players of all ages and backgrounds find solace in the intricate battle of wits it offers. From casual players engaging in friendly matches to competitive grandmasters participating in high-stakes tournaments, chess transcends boundaries and cultures. In the digital age, it has found new life and growth, with online platforms, streaming, and educational resources making it more accessible than ever. The sense of achievement derived from honing one's chess skills, along with the joy of outsmarting opponents, continues to draw in enthusiasts worldwide. In this visualisation, we will see the overall popularity of chess in the world today.

Density of Chess Players Across Countries

The map above shows the number of chess player across the world. We can see that the countries like Russia, India and Germany has the highest density of chess players compared to the countries around South Africa region which has lesser. This is due to the exposure of such countries about Chess and their facilities available for people to play them and be more recognised.

Age Distribution of Chess Players Across Countries

In this bubble chart we can see that most chess players are still below the age of 60. We can also observe that India has the youngest average chess players which is an average age of 27 year olds. This shows that some countries emphasise on providing exposure towards Chess at a very young age. This allows players to train and slowly climb up the reins of being Grandmaster.

Average FIDE Rankings of Players per Country

FIDE is the Fédération Internationale des Échecs also known as the World Chess Federation. This is the second most highest rank a chess player can obtain before the Grandmaster rank. The map shows the average FIDE ranking across all countries. We can see the countries that has lighter colour has higher FIDE ranking. Higher FIDE rankers can opt to be chess master and tutor and compete further for the title of Grandmaster. Cambodia has the highest average FIDE ranking compared to most countries around the world.
